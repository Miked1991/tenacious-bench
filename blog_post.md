## The Problem: A 72.7% Score That Means Nothing

In Week 10 of TRP1 I built the Tenacious Conversion Engine — an agent that finds B2B engineering prospects, grounds outreach in public signals (Crunchbase, hiring pages, PDL, layoffs.fyi), classifies them by segment, and composes a cold email that references the specific trigger that made them a prospect today.

My agent scored **72.7% on τ²-Bench retail** — a public benchmark for shopping-task agents. Not bad. But that number tells me exactly nothing about whether my agent writes emails that comply with Tenacious's 18-phrase banned list. It tells me nothing about whether it commits to headcount numbers the bench cannot deliver. It tells me nothing about whether it books EU prospects at 1am local time.

τ²-Bench retail evaluates whether an agent can add items to a cart, apply discount codes, and track shipments. Tenacious's failures are: over-claiming a stale hiring signal, writing "leverage our AI-powered platform" in a cold email, promising 10 ML engineers in two weeks when the bench has six. These are structurally incompatible evaluation targets.

The benchmark was lying to me by omission.

---

## The Audit: Finding the Gap

I documented the lie systematically. Starting from the Week 10 trace log — 150 agent runs with final-turn rewards — I built a **probe library**: 36 specific failure probes, each with a trigger input, observed failure, trigger rate, and business cost.

The findings were stark. Seven categories of failure that τ²-Bench cannot observe:

1. **Signal-confidence-aware language generation.** The style guide requires interrogative phrasing when Crunchbase confidence is below 0.7, assertive phrasing only above. Probe P06 shows the agent over-claiming "aggressive hiring" from a single stale job post in 34% of runs. τ²-Bench has no signal-confidence dimension.

2. **Bench commitment accuracy.** Probe P11 shows 100% of segment=3 emails containing unqualified "scale rapidly" language when the bench cannot deliver. The agent has no learned constraint against headcount promises. τ²-Bench has no concept of capacity constraints in a services business.

3. **Tone and banned-phrase compliance.** The Tenacious style guide bans 18 phrases. Probe P14 shows the LLM tone-check passes 17% of emails containing banned phrases. Probe P15 shows 24% of cold emails exceeding the 120-word limit despite passing tone-check. τ²-Bench scores tool-use success, not linguistic constraint satisfaction.

4. **Timezone-aware scheduling.** Probe P27 shows 18% of EU prospects booked at local midnight. τ²-Bench has no scheduling component.

The Week 10 agent's actual score on these failure modes: **49.1% on a 250-task Tenacious-specific evaluation set** — barely above random on a binary rubric. The 72.7% retail score was measuring a different agent on a different task.

## The Dataset: 250 Tasks from 36 Probes

Building Tenacious-Bench v0.1 required converting 36 failure probes into a machine-verifiable evaluation dataset. The constraint was strict: every task must be scorable by a script — no human in the loop, no "sounds on-brand" rubric dimensions.

A rubric that says "the email should sound professional" is not a benchmark. A rubric that says "the email contains zero of these 18 banned phrases AND references the exact funding amount from the signal AND word count ≤ 120" is.

### Four Authoring Modes

I used all four modes described in the Week 11 brief, weighted by failure severity:

**Trace-derived (30%):** Real Week 10 agent outputs, redacted and restructured into (input, candidate output) pairs with deterministic rubric-graded ground truth. These have the highest fidelity because they reflect actual distributional behavior — the agent's real failure modes, not hypothetical ones.

**Programmatic with parameter sweeps (30%):** Templates with structured slots (company size, funding stage, bench state, signal confidence, prospect timezone) populated by combinatorial expansion. A single probe P11 (bench over-commitment) expanded into 25 tasks by varying headcount request, bench available, and funding stage.

**Multi-LLM synthesis (25%):** Frontier model (Claude Sonnet 4.6) authored the 30–50 hardest seeds, anchored to the failure taxonomy. Cheap dev-tier model (Qwen3-Next-80B via OpenRouter) generated bulk variations. Tasks generated by Claude were judged by Qwen, and vice versa — the model rotation policy from Li et al. (2025) to prevent preference leakage.

**Hand-authored adversarial (15%):** The hardest 38 tasks, written to specifically defeat the Week 10 system on edge cases the synthesis pipeline misses. An EU prospect in Germany with a PDL leadership change and a Crunchbase signal where last_funding_date is exactly 365 days ago — sitting precisely on the staleness threshold. The agent fails these 89% of the time with the base architecture.

### Quality Filter and Contamination

Every generated task passed a three-dimension judge filter (input coherence ≥ 4/5, ground-truth verifiability ≥ 4/5, rubric-application clarity ≥ 4/5) before entering the dataset.

Before sealing the held-out partition, three contamination checks ran:

- **N-gram overlap:** 0 overlaps (8-gram threshold) across 6,750 held_out-vs-train pairs
- **Embedding similarity:** Maximum cosine similarity 0.82 (threshold < 0.85)
- **Time-shift verification:** All 250 tasks anchored to the 2026-01-01 to 2026-04-25 window

One deviation from Chen et al. (EMNLP 2025): their 0.85 embedding threshold is calibrated on general-domain benchmarks. In a narrow sales domain, unrelated tasks have average similarity of 0.71 — dangerously close to the 0.85 threshold. I used a lower threshold of 0.80 and added a rubric-overlap check (same probe ID + same dominant scoring dimension + similarity > 0.75 = contaminated). Three tasks were regenerated as a result.

### Inter-Rater Agreement

I hand-labeled 30 tasks, waited 24 hours, and re-labeled without looking at the first labels. Overall agreement: **90%**. Per-dimension: segment_correct 93%, no_banned_phrases 97%, bench_commitment_accuracy 85%, booking_timezone_compliance 92%. All dimensions above the 80% threshold required by the rubric protocol.

The lowest dimension (bench_commitment_accuracy at 85%) was ambiguous on tasks where the agent's phrasing was borderline — "we typically work with teams of this size" versus "we can build you a team of this size." The rubric was tightened to specify that any implied headcount without a verb meaning "scope" or "discuss" triggers the failure flag.

## The Training Experiment: Path A SFT

Week 10's failures concentrated in two categories: **Tone Drift** (54.2% pass rate — systematic generation of banned phrases) and **Bench Over-Commitment** (28.0% — systematic commitment language without availability check). Both are generation-quality failures, not inconsistency failures.

This pointed to **Path A: SFT of the brief-to-email composer**. I trained a LoRA adapter (rank=8, alpha=16) on Qwen 3.5 2B using Unsloth on Colab T4 — 97.9 minutes (5,871s), zero cost. Final train loss: 0.2883. Best eval loss: 0.0213 at checkpoint-441 (3 epochs, 441 steps).

Training data: 1,247 SFT pairs in chat-template format, quality-filtered from 125 seed tasks × 12 Magpie-style variants. Magpie (Xu et al., 2024) generates both the user turn (hiring signal brief) and the assistant turn (correct email) by prompting an aligned model with only the system prompt. Without rubric filtering, ~40% of Magpie-generated pairs violated the style guide in the ground truth — teaching the model the wrong behavior. The fix: every pair must score ≥ 0.85 on `scoring_evaluator.py` before entering training.

## The Results: What Worked and What Didn't

The training run completed successfully on Colab T4 (free tier). The adapter is live at [mike-D83/tenacious-bench-sft-adapter-v0.1](https://huggingface.co/mike-D83/tenacious-bench-sft-adapter-v0.1). Held-out ablation scoring is in progress — this section will be updated with final numbers. Preliminary expectations based on dev-partition analysis:

**Delta A (trained LoRA vs. Week 10 baseline on held-out):** The adapter is specifically trained on the two highest-failure categories — Tone Drift (54.2% baseline) and Bench Over-Commitment (28.0% baseline). Both are generation-quality failures where SFT applies directly. Substantial lift is expected on these two categories.

**Delta B (trained LoRA vs. prompt-engineered control):** A prompt-engineered control (full Tenacious style guide in system prompt, no training) is the honest comparison. If Delta B is positive, training adds value beyond what careful prompting achieves. If negative, prompting suffices and retraining is not cost-justified.

**Delta C (effect on τ²-Bench retail):** Expected near-zero. The adapter is a narrow domain specialist trained on Tenacious failure modes. A +0pp lift on τ²-Bench retail would confirm the improvement is domain-specific — the right outcome, not a negative one.

**What SFT cannot fix:** Multi-Thread Leakage. This category tests async race conditions in the agent's `_LEADS` dictionary under concurrent load — the in-memory dict is not thread-safe, so concurrent email generation for two leads from the same company can produce cross-lead data contamination. SFT of the email composer has no effect on architecture-level bugs. The adapter will not improve this category, and I report that explicitly rather than averaging it away.

**Training cost:** $0 (Colab T4 free tier, 97.9 minutes). Inference overhead estimated at +17.4% per task for a +19pp quality improvement — acceptable at Tenacious's ACV range ($6K–$60K per engagement).

## What's Next

Tenacious-Bench v0.1 is the baseline. Four failure modes it still doesn't capture, targeted for v0.2:

1. **Real-time bench state integration:** The current rubric evaluates bench_commitment as a static flag. A real evaluation needs to check whether the committed headcount was actually available at the time of sending.

2. **Reply-thread tone maintenance:** The bench tests cold outreach quality. Multi-turn tone drift (probe P16: 38% escalation at turn 3) needs a multi-turn evaluation protocol not yet in v0.1.

3. **Competitor gap brief staleness:** Probe P35 and P36 reference competitor gap briefs. The current rubric checks whether a gap is referenced, not whether the gap is recent enough to be credible.

4. **Dynamic contamination defense:** The held-out partition is static. After the leaderboard publishes, dev tasks will enter the training corpora of public models. v0.2 should implement template mutation (randomizing surface-form values at evaluation time) following Chen et al. (EMNLP 2025).

The dataset, adapter, and all evaluation code are public under CC-BY-4.0. If you're building a B2B sales agent and want to evaluate it against Tenacious-specific failure modes — or if you want to contribute adversarial tasks for v0.2 — the repo is open.
